How to redact a PDF

  1. 1

    Open your document

    Click "Select PDF File" or drag and drop your document onto the page. It loads instantly — nothing is uploaded anywhere.

  2. 2

    Draw over what you want to hide

    Click and drag to draw red rectangles over any names, numbers, addresses, or other details you want to remove. You'll see a red tinted box appear over each area.

  3. 3

    Check every page

    Use the page strip at the bottom to flip through your document. Pages with redactions show a red dot on their thumbnail. You can remove any box by hovering and clicking the X.

  4. 4

    Save the redacted PDF

    Click "Apply & Save." The sensitive content is permanently destroyed and a clean PDF downloads to your device. The hidden text cannot be recovered.

Frequently asked questions

Is the content actually removed, or just covered up?

Actually removed. Many "redaction" tools just place a black box on top of the text — someone can still copy-paste what's underneath. This tool completely re-renders each page as an image with the black boxes burned in. The original text is gone. It cannot be recovered, copied, selected, or searched.

Is my document safe? Who can see it?

Only you. Your file never leaves your computer — there's no upload, no cloud server involved. The redaction happens right inside your browser, making it safe for contracts, medical records, legal filings, and anything else confidential.

Can I undo a redaction?

Yes — before you save. Hover over any red box and click the X to remove it, or use the Undo button to remove the last one you drew. Once you click "Apply & Save," it's permanent.

Is it free?

Completely free. No account, no watermarks, no limits on how many PDFs you can redact.

Why not just highlight text in black using a regular PDF editor?

Because that doesn't actually hide anything. A black highlight is just a colored annotation sitting on top of the text. Anyone can remove it, copy the text underneath, or extract it with a simple tool. True redaction — like this tool does — destroys the content so it can never be seen again.

Can I still print the redacted document?

Yes. The redacted PDF opens and prints normally in any PDF viewer. Redacted areas will show up as solid black rectangles, exactly as you'd expect.
