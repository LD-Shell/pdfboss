About this tool

Got a contract, release form, or application that needs your signature? Draw it, type it, or upload an image of it — then drag it to exactly where it needs to go. No printing, no scanning, no fuss.

Three Ways to Sign

Draw freehand with your mouse or finger, type your name in a realistic handwriting font, or upload a photo of your existing signature.

How to sign a PDF

  1. 1

    Open your document

    Click "Select PDF File" or drag the document onto the page.

  2. 2

    Create your signature

    Draw it freehand, type your name and pick a handwriting font, or upload an image file of your signature.

  3. 3

    Position and save

    Drag your signature onto the right spot, resize it if needed, and click Save. The signed PDF downloads to your device instantly.

Frequently asked questions

Is this legally binding?

This tool places a visual signature onto the PDF. For most everyday purposes — agreements, approvals, acknowledgments — it works great. For formally legal contracts, check whether your jurisdiction requires a certified digital signature (like DocuSign). Many jurisdictions accept visual electronic signatures for most documents.

Can I sign multiple pages?

Yes. Navigate to any page using the page strip and place your signature wherever it's needed. You can sign as many pages as you want in the same document.

Can the recipient remove my signature?

The signature is permanently embedded into the page. It becomes part of the document and can't be moved, edited, or deleted by anyone after you save.

Is my signature stored anywhere?

No. Your signature and your document stay on your computer. Nothing is uploaded, logged, or stored on any server.

Is it free?

Completely free — no account, no watermarks, no limits on how many documents you sign.
