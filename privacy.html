100% Client-Side Processing

Most PDF websites work by forcing you to upload your sensitive documents to their remote servers. They process the file on their computer, and send the result back to you. This is a massive security risk.

PDFBoss is engineered differently. We use modern WebAssembly and JavaScript to process your files directly inside your web browser's local memory.

What this means for you:

  • Your files never leave your computer.
  • We do not operate servers, databases, or cloud storage capable of saving your files.
  • Even if PDFBoss was compromised, there are no user files to steal.
  • You can disconnect from the internet after loading the page, and the tools will still work perfectly.

Cookies & Local Data

We hate tracking cookies just as much as you do and we value your right to browse privately. PDFBoss does not use analytics software to monitor your clicks, mouse movements, or keystrokes.

We use a single piece of localStorage (a small temporary file saved in your browser) strictly to remember if you prefer Dark Mode or Light Mode.

Third-Party Ads (Google AdSense)

To keep these enterprise-grade tools free and cover our operational costs, we allow third-party vendors, including Google, to serve ads on our site.

  • Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on a user's prior visits to this website or other websites.
  • Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to our users based on their visit to our site and/or other sites on the Internet.
  • You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.

Important: These advertising scripts are strictly isolated. They cannot access, read, or upload the PDF files you process on our platform.

Network Infrastructure

While we do not host servers to process your PDFs, the application code itself must be delivered to your device. We utilize industry-standard Content Delivery Networks (CDNs) to load our web infrastructure securely.

Note: Modern browser security features (like Strict Tracking Prevention) may occasionally flag these CDNs. This is normal protocol to prevent cross-site cookies and does not impact the security of your documents.
