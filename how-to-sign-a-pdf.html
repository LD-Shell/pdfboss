Skip the print-sign-scan routine. Draw, type, or upload your signature and place it precisely on any PDF — in under a minute, entirely in your browser.

Why sign a PDF digitally?

Printing, signing by hand, and scanning back to PDF is slow, wastes paper, and requires a printer you may not always have access to. A digital signature embedded directly into a PDF is just as legally valid for most purposes and takes a fraction of the time. It's the standard approach for contracts, agreements, consent forms, and approval workflows.

3 ways to create your signature

PDFBoss gives you three methods — use whichever suits you:

  • Draw — use your mouse, trackpad, or finger on a touchscreen to draw your actual handwritten signature on a canvas.
  • Type — type your name and choose from three cursive fonts. Quick and clean for typed agreements.
  • Upload — photograph or scan your existing handwritten signature on white paper, crop it, and upload as a PNG. Works best with a transparent background.

Step-by-step: Sign a PDF with PDFBoss

Upload your PDF

Go to pdfboss.net/sign.html and upload the document you need to sign.

2

Create your signature

Choose the Draw, Type, or Upload tab and create your signature. You can change the pen colour in draw mode.

3

Place it on the document

Click Place on Document. Your signature appears as a draggable overlay on the PDF preview. Drag it to the signature line. Drag the blue corner handle to resize.

4

Save your signed PDF

Click Save PDF in the toolbar. Your signed document downloads immediately to your device.

Is a digital signature legally valid?

For most everyday documents — rental agreements, freelance contracts, consent forms, internal approvals — a signature image embedded in a PDF is widely accepted and legally sufficient. For high-stakes legal or financial documents requiring a cryptographically verified e-signature, you may need a dedicated e-signature service. When in doubt, check the requirements with the receiving party.

Tips for a professional-looking signature

  • If drawing on a laptop, use a slow, deliberate stroke — fast strokes look jagged
  • For the best draw result, use a touchscreen device with a stylus
  • If uploading a photo, use good lighting and a white background, then convert to PNG with a transparent background for the cleanest result
  • Scale the signature to match the size of the signature line on the form

Frequently asked questions

Can I sign on a specific page?

Yes — use the page navigation in the toolbar to move to the page you need to sign, then place your signature there.

Can I sign the same document multiple times?

Currently you can place one signature per save. To add multiple signatures, save after placing the first, then re-upload and add the second.

Does my signature get stored anywhere?

No. Your signature is created and used entirely in your browser session. Nothing is stored on any server.
